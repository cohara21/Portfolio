Mose

Student Project

YearWinter 2025
Duration10 weeks

Overview

Mose is a conceptual AI system designed to support dementia patients and their caregivers through empathetic guidance, memory reinforcement, and safety monitoring. Unlike standard home assistants, it focuses on personalized, natural interaction to reduce caregiver burden. By balancing proactive support with user independence, Mose helps individuals navigate their routines with confidence while providing families with peace of mind.

My Roles

Led UX research and design for the Mose system, including caregiver and patient touchpoints.
Tools: Figma, Photoshop, Rhino, Keyshot

Problem

Dementia creates a growing gap between independence and safety.

Dementia doesn't just affect memory, it erodes the autonomy of the individual and places an immense emotional burden on their family. For many patients, the fear of forgetting simple daily tasks leads to social withdrawal. Meanwhile, caregivers face constant anxiety and caregiver burnout as they struggle to monitor loved ones remotely.

Research

Understanding both sides of the story.

Our research focused on a dual-stakeholder approach. Through interviews with primary caregivers and health professionals, we identified a critical friction point: caregivers needed visibility without feeling like they were policing their loved ones,while patients needed guidance that felt like a supportive companion rather than a clinical monitor.

Solution

An ecosystem built for
support and connection.

The Mose App

Designed for the caregiver, the app provides real-time health insights and activity monitoring. It allows for the remote scheduling of reminders, giving family members peace of mind.

Home

The Mose Smart Device

The physical hardware serves as a gentle home companion for the patient.
Using adaptive AI, it provides personalized voice prompts and memory reinforcement through natural interaction.

The Mose Smart Device

Impact

Restoring dignity through intelligent assistance.

Mose successfully bridges the gap between independence and supervision. By automating daily reminders and safety checks, we reduced the cognitive load on patients and significantly lowered the stress levels of caregivers, allowing families to focus on quality time.
